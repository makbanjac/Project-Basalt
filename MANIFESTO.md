# Project Basalt — Manifesto

## Mission

**Building the physical layer for a digital generation.**

The internet gave a generation the means to earn a living from anywhere. It did not give them a place to live, a community to belong to, or infrastructure they actually own. Project Basalt closes that gap. We are building a remote-work intentional community whose members own the land they live on, the systems that power their homes, and the governance that runs their society.

We are not a co-living brand. We are not a retreat. We are not a movement. We are a working settlement under construction, run by the people who live in it.

## Who This Is For

Project Basalt is for high-skill remote workers and pioneers — engineers, builders, operators, designers, agronomists, medics, tradespeople — who want a measurable degree of autonomy from external systems: utilities, supply chains, housing markets, and eventually fiat currency.

We are not recruiting tourists. Membership requires labor, capital (modest), and consent to a governance system that places obligations on every participant.

## The Trajectory

Project Basalt evolves through four phases. Each phase has a function; we do not skip phases.

1. **Digital Cohort** — A distributed group on GitHub and Discord, drafting the OS and vetting members.
2. **The Pilot** — Temporary residence at the Alpha Site (Drinić, BiH) with grid-tied infrastructure (Starlink, generators, leased housing).
3. **Minimum Viable Infrastructure (MVI)** — Owned shelter, owned power, owned water, owned connectivity. Members can live on-site full-time.
4. **Autonomy** — Off-grid energy, food production, internal labor and credit markets. External income is a choice, not a requirement.

The thesis is simple: a community of remote workers can fund its own physical autonomy, in increments, by pooling external income against shared infrastructure. The bottleneck is not capital. It is governance, vetting, and the discipline to maintain what we build.

## The Operating Principles

### 1. Builders of our own Vision
We do not outsource the design of our society. Architecture, governance, legal structure, financial mechanics — these are products of the community, version-controlled and revised in the open. Members are not consumers of a vision authored by founders; they are co-authors. Every system in this repo is editable through the governance process defined in [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md).

### 2. Maintenance is equal to growth
A society that builds faster than it maintains collapses. Project Basalt treats upkeep — of infrastructure, of relationships, of governance documents, of land — as first-class work, compensated equivalently to new construction under the sweat-equity model in [ECONOMICS.md](ECONOMICS.md). Glamour work and grunt work earn the same hour.

### 3. Location-agnostic by design
The Alpha Site in Drinić is a starting point, not the project. Every system in this OS — governance, economics, vetting, exit — is written so it can be forked and instantiated at a second site without rewriting the core. Land is an input. The OS is the asset.

### 4. Transparent by default
Finances, decisions, member rosters (with consent), labor logs, and infrastructure status are visible to all members. Privacy from the outside world is fine; opacity to one another is not. Trust is engineered through verifiable records, not asserted through reputation.

### 5. Sweat is capital
The community begins with €2,000 of pooled cash and the labor of its founding members. Sweat equity is not a metaphor here — it is the primary mechanism by which ownership is acquired. See [ECONOMICS.md](ECONOMICS.md).

### 6. Autonomy is a gradient, not a binary
We do not pretend we can disconnect from the world overnight. Every quarter, we measure dependency on external systems (grid power, municipal water, fiat income, imported food) and publish the delta. Progress is measurable.

## What Project Basalt is Not

- **Not a commune** in the historical sense. Property is owned through a legal entity (the Community LLC or its jurisdictional equivalent); members hold shares; ownership is portable on exit.
- **Not a charity.** Members pay in — with labor, capital, or both. There is no free tier.
- **Not a sovereign-citizen project.** We comply with the laws of the host jurisdiction. Autonomy is from systems we choose to replace, not from legitimate authority.
- **Not a retreat for ideological homogeneity.** We select for competence, accountability, and consent to the governance system. Beliefs beyond that are the member's own.
- **Not permanent for everyone.** The Exit Clause in [ECONOMICS.md](ECONOMICS.md) treats leaving as a normal event, not a betrayal.

## The Commitment

A member of Project Basalt commits to three things:

1. **Contribute** — labor, capital, or both, as defined by the current phase.
2. **Govern** — participate in the sociocratic process; do not delegate your voice and then complain about outcomes.
3. **Maintain** — leave systems, relationships, and land in better condition than you found them.

Everything else is detail.
