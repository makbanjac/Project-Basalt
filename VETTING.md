# Project Basalt — Vetting Protocol

Most intentional communities fail on personality, not on infrastructure. This document defines the methodology the Community Circle uses to admit members. It is binding once ratified and amendable per §7 of [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md).

The protocol is deliberately demanding. We assume that **rejecting a marginal candidate costs the community far less than admitting one who is incompatible**. Rejection is reversible (the candidate can re-apply); admission, in practice, is not.

## 1. What We Are Selecting For

We screen against four explicit criteria. Every applicant is scored on each.

### 1.1 Demonstrated competence
The applicant produces verifiable output — code, designs, businesses run, structures built, organizations operated. We do not score credentials; we score artifacts. A senior engineer with no GitHub history and no portfolio is harder to vet than a self-taught builder with five years of public commits. The artifact, not the title, is the evidence.

### 1.2 Capital readiness
The applicant can, without distress, contribute the minimum required for their target member class (defined in [BUDGET.md](BUDGET.md) and updated per phase). "Without distress" means the contribution does not consume the applicant's emergency reserves or essential income. We do not admit members whose participation would put them in financial harm — that pressure becomes governance pressure within months.

### 1.3 Governance literacy
The applicant has read and can articulate the working logic of [MANIFESTO.md](MANIFESTO.md), [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md), [ECONOMICS.md](ECONOMICS.md), and [BUDGET.md](BUDGET.md). They can describe a paramount objection, the difference between consent and consensus, and the conditions of the Override Protocol. This is the lowest-cost criterion to test and the best leading indicator of low-friction membership.

### 1.4 Conduct & temperament
The applicant has a track record of completing what they commit to, handling disagreement without escalation, and maintaining relationships across long timelines. This is the hardest criterion to verify quickly and the one we spend the most effort on.

## 2. The Application Funnel

Applications proceed through five stages. A candidate may be declined at any stage. The Community Circle records the stage of decline and the substantive reason in the (member-only) vetting log.

### Stage 1 — Written Application (asynchronous)

Submitted via a public form linked from the repository README. Required fields:

1. **Identity & basics** — name, jurisdiction, age, languages, current employment status.
2. **Artifacts** — links to ≥3 pieces of work the applicant authored or co-authored. Code, writing, structures, businesses, organizations. Each accompanied by a 2-sentence description of the applicant's specific contribution.
3. **Skills inventory** — checklist plus free-text. The current Phase's skill needs are advertised on the application page; gaps are not disqualifying but are noted.
4. **Capital position** — a self-attested statement of the contribution band the applicant is targeting (per [BUDGET.md](BUDGET.md) §1). Numbers are not collected; bands are. Applicants commit to a band, not a balance.
5. **Governance check** — five short questions verifying they have read and understood the OS. Answers are graded.
6. **Conflict scenario** — a 200-word free response to a written conflict scenario provided on the form. Scored for reasoning, not for "correct" answer.
7. **References** — two named references, at least one professional. Contact details collected; references are contacted only at Stage 3.
8. **Why this** — a 300-word answer to: *"What about your life makes a project like Project Basalt the right next move, and what about it would you find difficult?"* The second clause is mandatory; applications that only address the first are returned for revision.

**Decision at Stage 1:** Two members of the Community Circle independently score the application. A pass requires both reviewers to advance it. Disagreements are resolved by a third reviewer. Target turnaround: 14 days.

### Stage 2 — First Conversation (60–90 minutes, video)

Conducted by two Community Circle members. Structured, not free-form, to prevent reviewer drift and bias.

Agenda:

1. **Walkthrough of one artifact.** The applicant explains a project they listed in Stage 1, including what failed, what they would change, who else was involved.
2. **Lived-week reconstruction.** The applicant describes the past 7 days hour-by-hour. This surfaces actual habits, not stated ones, with very low coaching potential.
3. **Disagreement probe.** "Tell us about the last time you held a position your team disagreed with. What happened?" Followed by 2–3 follow-up questions. Looking for: specificity, accountability for outcome, ability to describe the other side fairly.
4. **Open questions from the applicant.** This is also evaluated — the questions an applicant asks reveal what they actually care about.

**Decision at Stage 2:** Both interviewers must advance. Detailed notes are filed. Target turnaround: 7 days from Stage 1 pass.

### Stage 3 — Reference Checks

The Community Circle contacts both references with a short structured interview (15–25 minutes each). Questions:

1. How long and in what context have you known the applicant?
2. Describe a project you saw them complete. What did they do well? What did they do poorly?
3. Describe a conflict or setback you saw them handle. How did they handle it?
4. Would you work with them again? In what role would you not work with them?
5. Is there anything you wish we had asked?

Reference inputs are weighted, not determinative. A reference who cannot describe a setback or a limitation is treated with skepticism — the world's best applicant has both.

**Decision at Stage 3:** Community Circle reviews all reference notes together and decides by consent to advance. Target turnaround: 14 days.

### Stage 4 — Trial Membership

A pass at Stage 3 admits the applicant as a **Trial Member** for **90 days minimum**, with the following structure:

- **Embed in one Circle.** The Trial Member participates in one operational Circle's meetings, takes ownership of at least one task per month, and is included in the Circle's working channels.
- **Sweat hours logged from day one.** Hours count toward share issuance retroactive to admission *if* the member completes the trial.
- **Capital contribution begins.** At 50% of full-member rate during the trial. This avoids buyer's remorse on either side.
- **Two scheduled check-ins** — at day 30 and day 75 — between the Trial Member and their assigned Community Circle sponsor. Either party can flag concerns.
- **In-person time required** — minimum 7 days at the Alpha Site or with founding members during the trial, in one or more visits. (Phase 0 exception: deferred until Phase 1 if no site is yet operational. Replaced with a distributed multi-day working session.)

The Trial period is the single highest-signal stage. Most decline outcomes that should happen, happen here. The Community Circle is empowered to end the trial at any time with stated reasons; the Trial Member is empowered to withdraw at any time with no penalty.

### Stage 5 — Full Membership

At day 90+, the General Circle decides by consent on Full Membership. Inputs:

- The Community Circle sponsor's written report.
- The trial Circle Lead's written report.
- The Trial Member's own self-assessment (1 page, structured).

A pass admits the member to full standing per [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md) §5.2. A decline ends the trial; the candidate may re-apply after 12 months, with the prior decline visible to the next reviewing Circle.

## 3. Reviewer Discipline

Vetting is corrupted by reviewer fatigue, friendship effects, and pattern-matching to the reviewer's own profile. Mitigations:

- **Two-reviewer rule** at every gated stage. Single-reviewer advances are not allowed.
- **Structured forms.** Reviewers fill scoring rubrics, not free-form impressions.
- **Conflict declaration.** A reviewer who has worked with, dated, owes money to, or is owed money by an applicant declares it and recuses.
- **Calibration session quarterly.** The Community Circle reviews 2–3 past decisions (passes and declines) against actual outcomes, and updates the rubric.
- **No "founder veto" outside Circle process.** Founding members do not have asymmetric authority in vetting; the protocol applies to friends and strangers identically.

## 4. What Gets an Application Declined Quickly

The following are immediate declines, recorded with one-line rationale:

- **Misrepresentation of artifacts** — claims authorship of work the applicant did not author.
- **Pattern of unilateral action** — the applicant's history shows them taking irreversible actions without consultation in collaborative settings.
- **Refusal to engage with the OS** — applicant treats governance as bureaucratic obstacle rather than as the project's substance.
- **Pressure on capital readiness** — applicant signals the contribution will strain them, or asks for the requirement to be waived.
- **Hostility to maintenance work** — the applicant's stated interests are exclusively in greenfield, glamour, or "vision" work, with no interest in upkeep. The Manifesto's principle on maintenance is non-negotiable.

These criteria are written down here so they can be applied without case-by-case argument.

## 5. Special Cases

### 5.1 Founding members
Founding members (Phase 0 cohort) are not vetted via this protocol — they wrote it. However, Phase 0 members are subject to a **mutual review at the start of Phase 1**, where each founding member is consented into Full Membership by the others. A founding member who does not pass mutual review is not removed; they are converted to advisor status with no equity dilution and no governance vote. This is the price of having authored the system from inside it.

### 5.2 Couples and family units
Couples may apply jointly. Each adult is vetted independently and held to the full protocol. Joint application does not lower the bar for either partner. Children are not vetted; their inclusion is a Community Circle decision that considers safety, schooling, and the cohort's current capacity.

### 5.3 Capital-only members
Members whose primary contribution is cash (defined as ≥80% of their stake from cash) are vetted on the same four criteria. The competence and capital-readiness checks are easier; the governance and conduct checks are identical. Capital is not a fast-track; capital with poor governance temperament has destroyed more communities than insufficient funding.

### 5.4 Re-applicants
A previously declined applicant may re-apply after 12 months. The new application proceeds through all stages from Stage 1, but reviewers have access to the prior decline rationale. Re-applicants who address the specific reasons for prior decline are weighted favorably.

## 6. Privacy & Data

- Application materials are visible only to the Community Circle.
- Reference call notes are visible only to the Community Circle and destroyed after the decision unless the applicant becomes a member.
- Decline rationales are recorded in the (member-only) vetting log with the applicant's identifying details redacted to initials after 12 months.
- We do not publish member rosters externally without each member's explicit, revocable consent.

## 7. Versioning

This protocol is iterative. Every fourth completed cohort triggers a calibration review per §3, and amendments follow the standard process in [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md) §7.
