# Project Basalt — Budget & Capital Plan

This document is the financial reality check. It models what each phase actually costs, where the money realistically comes from given the founding cohort's earning capacity, and the gap between the two. It is updated quarterly by the Finance & Legal Circle.

All figures are working estimates in EUR. Hard prices come from quoted vendors and are committed to the ledger when received.

## 1. Sizing Reality: The Pooling Capacity

A founding cohort of remote workers in the broader Western Balkans / Eastern European market typically earns net **€1,400–€3,500/month**. This is the relevant range for Project Basalt's recruitment pool — not Bay Area or Berlin remote salaries, which represent a small fraction of available candidates.

Realistic pooling rules of thumb (per member, sustained over 12 months without burning personal reserves):

| Net monthly income | Personal living costs | Realistic monthly contribution to Basalt |
|---|---|---|
| €1,400–1,800 | €700–900 | €150–300 |
| €1,800–2,500 | €800–1,100 | €300–600 |
| €2,500–3,500 | €900–1,300 | €600–1,000 |
| €3,500+ | €1,000–1,500 | €1,000–2,000 |

A founding cohort of **5 members in the lower-to-mid bands** pools roughly **€10,000–25,000/year** in cash, plus sweat hours. This is the working assumption for Phase 1 budgeting. Anything beyond requires either (a) a higher-income recruit, (b) early revenue activity, or (c) external financing.

This frames every number below.

### 3.4 Funding reality

A 5-member cohort pooling at the **lower band (€200/month/member)** generates **€12,000/year**. That covers a no-vehicle Phase 1 in **roughly 11–14 months of saving** before the pilot can begin.

**Implication:** Phase 1 is feasible without external capital, but only if the cohort commits to a **disciplined 12-month savings runway** before stepping on-site. Attempting Phase 1 sooner means under-equipping, and under-equipped Pilots fail.

**Faster paths to Phase 1, in order of realism:**

1. **One mid-band member (~€600/month contribution)** added to a 4-member lower-band cohort — Phase 1 fundable in ~7 months.
2. **Early revenue activity** in Phase 0: a small consulting/dev pod inside the cohort billing externally, with a portion routed to the LLC. €500–1,500/month of pod revenue cuts the runway materially.
3. **Founding-member loans to the LLC** with documented repayment terms (treated as debt, not equity, to avoid distorting the cap table).

What we will **not** do: take on external debt, accept grants with conditions, or admit a high-capital member without standard vetting. Speed is not worth governance risk.

## 4. Phase 2 — Minimum Viable Infrastructure

This is where the budget jumps an order of magnitude. Phase 2 is not fundable from cohort-pooled cash alone at the current income profile and would not be honest to model that way.

### 4.1 Indicative line items (per dwelling and per shared system)

| System | Range (EUR) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tiny home / cabin (30–40 m², weather-tight, 4-season) | €15,000–40,000 | DIY-with-local-labor low end; pre-fab high end. |
| Solar PV + storage (3–5 kW with 10 kWh battery) | €8,000–15,000 | Per dwelling or per cluster. |
| Well drilling (depth-dependent in Drinić karst) | €3,000–10,000 | Karst geology in this region is highly variable; budget high. |
| Septic / greywater system | €2,000–5,000 | |
| Site prep, foundations, access road | €5,000–20,000 | Slope, soil, distance to road drive this. |
| Shared kitchen / common building | €10,000–25,000 | Often the social heart; under-investing here is regretted. |
| Wood storage, workshop, tool shed | €3,000–8,000 | |
| Building permits, surveys, engineering | €2,000–6,000 | |
| **Per-dwelling envelope (own + share)** | **€35,000–80,000** | |
| **5-dwelling MVI total** | **€175,000–400,000** | |

### 4.2 Funding paths for Phase 2

At cohort pooling alone (€12,000–25,000/year), Phase 2 is a **7–15 year horizon**. That is honest, and it is too slow. Realistic paths to compress that:

1. **Cohort growth to 10–15 members during Phase 1**, with at least 2–3 in higher-income bands.
2. **Capital members:** admit members whose primary contribution is cash (€20–80k each), with shares issued at standard valuation. This must happen *after* Phase 1 has demonstrated competence — otherwise you are selling shares in a fantasy.
3. **Revenue activity:** the "Remote Work Agency" arc from the Manifesto. A pod billing €5–15k/month externally, with 20–30% routed to the LLC as services revenue, generates €15–55k/year of LLC capital.
4. **Asset-backed borrowing:** once the LLC owns land outright, modest borrowing against it (≤30% LTV) becomes possible. Used cautiously.
5. **Phased construction:** build one dwelling fully, prove the spec, then the next. Do not parallel-build five half-finished cabins.

A realistic Phase 2 trajectory: **24–48 months from Phase 1 start**, assuming at least two of the four mechanisms above are activated.

## 5. Phase 3 — Autonomy

Not budgeted in this version. Phase 3 cost modeling is a Phase 2 deliverable, because the input variables (food self-sufficiency, energy baseline, member count) are unknown until Phase 2 is operational.

## 6. The Honest Gap

| Phase | Cost range | Cohort pooling capacity | Self-fundable? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 0 | €2,100–3,700 | High | **Yes** |
| Phase 1 | €10,700–15,600 | Medium (12-month runway) | **Yes, with discipline** |
| Phase 2 | €175,000–400,000 | Insufficient alone | **No** — requires growth + revenue + capital members |
| Phase 3 | TBD | TBD | TBD |

**Conclusion.** Project Basalt can begin and reach Phase 1 entirely on the founding cohort's own resources. Reaching Phase 2 requires either (a) substantially expanding the cohort, (b) building a revenue arm during Phase 1, or both. The OS is not a fantasy at Phase 0 or Phase 1. The OS becomes a fantasy if Phase 2 is attempted before the funding mechanism is real.

The Finance & Legal Circle's primary job during Phase 1 is therefore **not to manage a small treasury** but to **engineer the Phase 2 funding mechanism**: the agency arc, the capital-member valuation policy, and the legal vehicle for taking external capital cleanly.
